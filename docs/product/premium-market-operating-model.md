# Premium market operating model

## Position

**Interior care for exceptional properties.**

Lake & Pine plans detail-led cleaning for private estates, construction handoffs,
lake and marine interiors, and select professional spaces. The common system is a
defined scope, property-specific care notes, capacity-aware scheduling, documented
closeout, and accountable service recovery.

The public campaign line is **From final walkthrough to ready-for-arrival.**

Rapid-turnover vacation-rental work is not the focus. Do not use "luxury" as a
substitute for operating proof; the premium experience has to come from planning,
continuity, careful scope control, communication, and follow-through.

## Priority customers and programs

1. **Private Estate Care** — large primary homes, seasonal residences, household
   managers, executive assistants, family offices, and estate stewards. Programs can
   include an initial reset, recurring care, arrival/departure preparation, seasonal
   opening/closing, and event resets when trained capacity exists.
2. **Construction Handoff** — custom builders, remodelers, designers, project
   managers, and premium real-estate teams. Programs can include rough clean, detailed
   final clean, pre-walkthrough touch-up, renovation-dust reset, and owner handoff.
3. **Lake & Marine Interior Care** — boat owners, marinas, and lake-concierge
   partners. Initial scope is cabins, galleys, heads, interior surfaces, linens, and
   arrival/departure preparation. It excludes hulls, gelcoat, mechanical spaces,
   bilges, coatings, and remediation unless separately qualified.
4. **Select Commercial Care** — design, architecture, legal, finance, premium retail,
   showrooms, model homes, marina offices, and similar ordinary professional spaces.
   Medical, clinical, industrial, manufacturing, restaurant, and regulated sanitation
   work is out of scope until separately qualified.

## Service boundaries

Explicit non-fit work:

- rapid-turnover vacation rentals;
- biohazard, trauma, hoarding, mold, pest, or other remediation;
- active hazardous construction debris or sites without safe access/utilities;
- industrial/manufacturing cleanup or clinical sanitation;
- restaurant kitchens or food-production sanitation;
- exterior marine detailing, coatings, hull correction, or mechanical service; and
- any project without a reviewed scope and confirmed operating capacity.

Never claim licensing, bonding, insurance, screening, certifications, same-crew
guarantees, marine-safe chemicals, service coverage, response times, customer counts,
or review volume until the claim has a maintained evidence record.

## Request-to-service lifecycle

The website accepts a **request**, never an instant confirmed appointment.

```text
requested
  -> needs_information | walkthrough_needed | reviewing
  -> proposal_sent
  -> approved
  -> scheduled
  -> en_route
  -> in_progress
  -> quality_review
  -> completed
  -> issue_reported
  -> reclean_scheduled
  -> resolved | refund_review | closed
```

Cancellation can occur from any pre-service state subject to the approved service
agreement. Refund requests create a review record; they never initiate live money from
an unauthenticated public request.

## Intelligent scheduling

Hard constraints must all pass:

- cleaner/team is qualified for the service program;
- service territory and travel boundary permit the job;
- safe access, utilities, site/dock readiness, and operating hours are compatible;
- minimum crew size, duration, shift, and travel buffers fit;
- finish/product restrictions can be honored;
- construction phase or vessel location is compatible; and
- no availability/time-off conflict exists.

Soft scoring ranks feasible options:

- preserve known crew/property continuity;
- match finish, property, construction, or marine-interior experience;
- prefer the customer's first-choice window, then alternatives;
- minimize travel and preserve recurring route consistency;
- respect owner-arrival, walkthrough, handoff, or event deadlines;
- balance workload and retain a quality/weather buffer; and
- prioritize service-recovery work without destabilizing confirmed customers.

The scheduler recommends options with reasons. An operator confirms complex work.

## Vertical intake

- **Estate:** occupancy/seasonality, size and rooms, finish sensitivities, pets,
  household coordination, access, linens, cadence, privacy/photo permission, primary
  and alternate dates.
- **Construction:** project phase, finished area, builder/GC contact, handoff deadline,
  utilities, debris/site readiness, active trades, finish types, access, parking, and
  walkthrough requirements.
- **Marine:** vessel length/type, cabins, galley/head, dock/trailer/storage location,
  shore power/water/access, interior materials, hazard/remediation screening, and owner
  arrival/launch timing.
- **Commercial:** facility type/size, operating hours, restrooms, traffic, access/alarm,
  frequency, floor/finish types, consumables, security restrictions, and preferred
  service windows.

Exact street addresses, access codes, alarm details, payment data, and private household
notes should be collected only after they are necessary and through an appropriately
protected channel.

## Service recovery and money boundary

The product supports reschedule, cancellation, complaint, re-clean, credit, damage
incident, and refund-review records with an immutable event trail. A customer request
does not promise an outcome and does not move money.

Before publishing exact deadlines or fees, the founder must approve the operating
policy and obtain appropriate legal review. A reasonable draft for review is:

- appointment exists only after written confirmation;
- scope changes require written approval;
- customer can request a reschedule/cancellation and see its status;
- missed-scope complaints create a quality-review case tied to the service record;
- a focused re-clean is the preferred first remedy when safe and appropriate;
- credits/refunds require an authorized decision and, once payments exist, are returned
  through the original payment channel; and
- unsafe site, weather, marina closure, or company cancellation never penalizes the
  customer.

## Product test

Every feature should answer at least one of these questions better:

1. Is this property and scope a fit?
2. What does the operator need to price and plan it safely?
3. Which qualified team and feasible window should be recommended, and why?
4. What was agreed, completed, changed, or missed?
5. What action is owed next, by whom, and by when?
